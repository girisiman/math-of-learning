Mathematics of Learning: Mathematical Interpretations of Machine Learning Algorithms.

General Overview

This course offers a mathematically rigorous introduction to the foundations of machine learning, focusing on the theoretical principles that govern learning from data. It develops a deep understanding of key concepts such as empirical risk minimization, PAC learning, VC dimension, Rademacher complexity, information-theoretic measures, and optimization techniques‚Äîall essential to analyze the performance, reliability, and limitations of modern learning algorithms.

While the topics covered are not always directly applied in day-to-day machine learning practice, they form a vital intellectual foundation for anyone seeking a principled understanding of the field. These are the conceptual tools‚Äîthe "arrows in your quiver"‚Äîthat will enrich your ability to reason about algorithms, grasp advanced topics in learning theory, and navigate future innovations in artificial intelligence and data science.

The course emphasizes proofs, derivations, and conceptual clarity over implementation, encouraging students to engage with the why behind learning methods, not just the how. Although rooted in advanced theory, the course has been thoughtfully designed to be accessible to undergraduate students with a strong interest in mathematics, statistics, and computer science. It builds up ideas incrementally and rigorously, making it an ideal stepping stone toward graduate-level machine learning and research.
